Belly

Inzombia

Review

  • 3 days ago
  •  2,174
  •  1,423

Belly’s most recent project titled “Inzombia” which came out 11th November has been one of the top projects in my playlist since its release topping Meek Mills “Dreamchasers 4”. Once again just like with “Up for Days” & “Another Day In Paradise” Belly produces a brilliant tape that can get played from start to finish without skipping tracks. The project includes a total of 11 tracks.

MUSE Review

“Insomia turned me into a zombie” a line from the track titled “Die Alone” is most probably the line that helped him title his current project because he is basically saying that a lack of sleep is turning him into a zombie like person. I wondered why he came up with the title but in this track he talks on how he separated himself from this girl for quite a long time, until he no longer felt any pain.

Belly loves his drugs as you’ll be able to tell when listening to some of songs. In “The Day That I Met You” where he talks about the day he met his ex-girl he says “Drug diet, drug diet, we had chemistry baby, I love science The love dying, the love dying, sippin' purple rain till I hear the dubs cry”.

Although the Mixtape can be played straight through from beginning to end I still have my favourite tracks that I go to first. These are “Frozen Water”, “Re Up” & “Consuela”. The track “Frozen Water” has a catchy hook sang by Belly with future putting in few adlibs in the background. “Re up” includes a verse from Hot in the scene Nav who’s been ever present in the last 10 months with some brilliant releases such as “Nothing On Me” and “TTD (Ten Toes Down)”. Lastly “Consuela” which features Thugga Thugga (Young Thug) is about a girl from Venezuela and her lifestyle with drugs due to her upbringing in the slums.

With 8k downloads and over 47,000 plays on Datpiff so far in the 3 days since release the project has been received quite well by his fans and listeners. If you’re a fan of Belly and haven’t listened to his project yet make sure to head over to Datpiff to download & support him or alternatively stream & purchase his tape below and leave your comments

Speaker Review

JBL Charge 2+

Home Devices

  • 3 days ago
  •  2,174
  •  1,423

The JBL Charge 2+ Wireless Bluetooth Speaker offers a massive amount of perks to its users. As an owner of this wonderful designed device I can say it was well worth its money. I’ve had the speaker since the turn of the year and it’s been a blessing I must say. What I was most impressed with about the speaker was the rich and strong sound the speaker offers. From first playback of my one of my favourite artists J. Cole I was impressed to hear how the speaker offered such an immersive sound allowing me to feel the vibrations of the bass. We all know bass is what makes us say whether a set of speakers or headphones are worth the purchase. Even when played at full volume (which I do quite a lot and im sure you will to) the JBL Charge 2+ still offers quality sound with very little distortion.

The sound emmited from the speaker mainly comes out through the hard plastic speaker grille. This covers much of the front and back of the speaker with sound emitting from the former. From the front speaker you’ll mostly here the vocal side to your songs with the best part of the design in my opinion on either end. This is where you hear, feel and even see the bass. It comes designed with two 1.5-inch drivers on each end which each include Passive Radiators which allows us to see them actually trembling. I must say this feature is what will make this device a must for us music lovers.

Besides the sound quality the speaker still hosts a number a quality perks I still have to mention. The second most notable one is the battery life. Now you would hate to have such quality sound, but you cant even listen to your tunes/bangerz for the desired amount of time; well no in this case. The JBL Charge 2+ gives us an estimated 12-hour battery life with the bulit in rechargeable 6000mAH Battery. That can also give charge to your other devices such as a smart phone or tablet via its USB port loacted on the back of the device.

Some of the other quality perks the speaker hosts are onces such as Social Mode and Splashproof design. Starting with the former Social Mode is new feature that many of us wouldn’t have come across before. When this mode is activated via the row panel on the top of the speaker the JBL Charge 2+ allows up to 3 users to connect simultaniously via Bluetooth connectivity. This mode somewhat brings out the DJ in everyone as it allows any device connected to have control over what song is going to play. Kinda brings back that old school feeling of when you had the bangerz in the car with the aux cord; Instead this time its via bluetooth and come happen in any location.

As with anything in life. There is always the good and the bad. Although this speaker boasts a host of pros there are still some of the negative aspects that have to be addressed. Firstly there is one aspect that can be considered both good & bad, depending on your take. Althrough the design is sleek & simple its shape reminds you of a very large energy drink can. This can lead to difficulties placing it into small bags such as pounches or handbags. However the designs does bring a positive and this is that the speaker is very durable and can withstand drops of tables or clumsy fingers. So its down to the you with that one.

There are though a few faults that I have noticed from my interaction with the speaker. These problems are both with the bluetooth connectivity. On some occasions I have had to disconnect and forget device because it just wont connect although this has only happened twice since I’ve owned the JBL Charge 2+. The last problem is that the Bluetooth Connection does not require a password. Having a password would slow down the connecting phase but in my opinion I think it would stop the possibility of others say at a party or gathering from going up to your speaker and just connecting their device hassle free. The cons that I have included are something that had to be noted on none the less a great speaker.

Overall I would say that the JBL Charge 2+ is a must purchase. Life includes many sacrafices in order to gain and getting the Charge 2+ is similar to that anaolgy. You may have to sacrafice the small bag to carry it but and if you care about the quality of your sound it’s a sacrafice well worth making. Im 99.99% sure you wont regret it.

Album Comparison Review

Chip - Power Up
Wretch 32 - Growing Over Life


Power Up – 6/10
Growing over Life 8.5/10

Chip and Wretch 32 have released albums only a few days apart , so naturally we had to compare and review them both. Now Chip has been off the scene for a while, besides the odd diss track and fire in the booth video, his musical presence has been non-existent. When I saw Power Up on Apple music I was egar to hear this long over due album, I was actually excited for his come back.

At first glance I wasn’t too impressed by the features and was kind of disappointed that it was only 8 tracks long! I was just hoping the tracks are vibe worthy. The stand out tracks for me and the ones I would recommend skipping to are Mad with it, My Ones ft Ms D and Style Dat ft Kranium, However the rest of the album was nothing to get to excited about and ill propberly forget about it a week or so. Growing over life on the other hand is something to get excited about. I honestly forget about Wretch from time to time, his talented but often slept on in my opinion. No one can sleep on Wretch after releasing growing over life, the whole album is a vibe, I was hooked from the first track Antwi, and the rest of the album did not disappoint! My top Tracks have to be Antwi, Take me as I am , Liberation, Open conversation and Cooked Food. The rest of the album still bangs and I would suggest listening from start to finish, but these songs are my personal favourite’s and often get pulled up mid song.

Honestly Power up sounds childish in comparison to Growing Over Life. Wretch 32 just gives us more of a grown up sound. I was really routing for Chips come back and I wanted to love Power up , but Growing over Life is a 12 track album that I can play all day everyday and not get bored! How can you fault an album like that.
